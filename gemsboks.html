GEMSBOK

The gemsbok or gemsbuck (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope in the Oryx genus, native to the arid regions of Southern Africa, including the Kalahari Desert. Some authorities formerly included the East African oryx as a subspecies of the gemsbok.

The gemsbok is featured on Namibia's coat of arms, and the current estimated population in the country is around 373,000 individuals.

The name "gemsbok" comes from Afrikaans, derived from the Dutch word for the male chamois. Despite some visual similarities in facial patterns, the chamois and the oryx are not closely related.
